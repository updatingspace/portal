## 2. Who this service is for

The service is intended for users aged **13+**.

If you are under the age at which you can validly consent to optional privacy-sensitive features under the law applicable to you, we may require consent from a parent or legal guardian for those optional features.

## 3. What data we collect

Depending on how you use the service, we may process:

- account and identity data: email address, username, display name, first name, last name, approval/application status, tenant membership status;
- authentication and security data: session identifiers, passkey/webauthn completion state, MFA-related state, IP address, request identifiers, security and audit logs;
- profile and community data: biography, avatar/media metadata, community memberships, team memberships, roles and permissions;
- content you create: posts, comments, reactions, uploads, event participation, RSVP/attendance data, polls, nominations, votes, achievement grants and similar community actions;
- external account-linking data: connector settings, external account identifiers such as Steam account references, and normalized activity derived from linked accounts;
- technical/browser data: cookie values required to maintain a session, CSRF tokens, device/browser metadata reasonably necessary for security and delivery.

We do **not** intentionally request special categories of personal data. Please do not publish such data through community content features.

## 4. Where we get data from

We collect data:

- directly from you;
- from tenant/community administrators who approve or manage access;
- from linked third-party services when you choose to connect them;
- automatically from your browser or device when needed to secure and deliver the service.

## 5. Why we process your data and the GDPR legal bases

We process personal data for the following purposes:

| Purpose | Examples | Legal basis |
|---|---|---|
| Provide the service | account creation after approval, login, session management, profile, communities, events, voting, achievements | contract / steps at your request |
| Keep the platform secure | fraud prevention, abuse handling, rate limiting, audit logs, incident response | legitimate interests |
| Moderate communities and enforce rules | anti-abuse review, suspension/ban handling, content moderation | legitimate interests |
| Optional external account linking | linking Steam or other connectors, syncing optional external activity | consent |
| Legal and compliance obligations | respond to rights requests, maintain records, incident reporting, Russian data protection obligations | legal obligation |

Where we rely on consent, you may withdraw it at any time. Withdrawal does not affect processing already carried out lawfully before withdrawal.

See the separate [Cookie Notice](./cookie-notice.md).

## 7. Who receives your data

Your data may be disclosed to:

- infrastructure providers and processors used to host and secure the service;
- tenant/community administrators, but only within the scope of their roles and permissions;
- authorities or third parties where required by law or to protect rights, security or the service;
- other users, where you choose to publish content or use community features designed for visibility within a tenant, community or team.

The current processor baseline includes:

- **Yandex Cloud** for Russian-hosted infrastructure;
- **self-hosted infrastructure under the controller’s direct control**.

The maintained processor list is documented internally in the [Processor / Subprocessor Register](./processors-register.md).

## 8. International transfers

The current production footprint includes infrastructure in **Russia**, and a separate **EEA-hosted deployment** is planned.

If personal data subject to the GDPR is transferred outside the EEA to a country without an adequacy decision, we will rely on an applicable transfer mechanism under Chapter V GDPR and provide information about the safeguard used on request.

## 9. How long we keep data

We keep data only for as long as necessary for the purposes described above, subject to security, legal and dispute-related needs.

Operational retention rules are set out in the [Data Retention Schedule](./retention-schedule.md).

## 10. Your rights

Depending on applicable law, you may have the right to:

- access your personal data;
- correct inaccurate or incomplete data;
- erase data;
- restrict processing;
- object to certain processing;
- receive a portable copy of data you provided to us where the law grants that right;
- withdraw consent where processing is based on consent;
- lodge a complaint with a competent supervisory authority.

You can contact us at **privacy@updspace.com**. We may need to verify your identity before acting on a request.

## 11. Rights response timing

- GDPR requests: we aim to respond without undue delay and normally within **one month**.
- Russian-law requests: we aim to respond within the shorter operational/statutory timeframe applicable to the request type.

The internal handling process is described in the [DSAR Procedure](./dsar-procedure.md).

## 12. Children

The service is not intended for children under 13.

If we learn that we have collected personal data from a child in violation of applicable law, we will delete or otherwise lawfully remediate that data without undue delay.

## 13. Security

We use technical and organizational measures appropriate to the risk, including tenant isolation, signed internal service-to-service calls, cookie-based sessions, access control, audit logging and retention controls.

No online service can promise absolute security.

We may update this notice from time to time. The latest version will be published with its effective date.
